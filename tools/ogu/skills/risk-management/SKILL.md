# Risk Management

## When to Use
- Planning a major migration or architectural change
- Evaluating a build vs buy decision with significant trade-offs
- Preparing a risk register for a compliance audit

## Workflow
1. Identify risks: what could prevent success or cause harm (technical, organizational, external)
2. Assess each risk: likelihood (1-5) × impact (1-5) = risk score
3. Prioritize: focus mitigation effort on high-score risks
4. Define mitigations: specific actions that reduce likelihood or impact
5. Review register monthly; close resolved risks, add new ones as context changes

## Quality Bar
- Risk register is a living document, not a one-time artifact
- Every high-risk item has an owner and a mitigation plan
- Residual risk after mitigation is explicitly acknowledged and accepted by stakeholders
- Risks are communicated to decision-makers before they materialize
