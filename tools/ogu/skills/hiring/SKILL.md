# Hiring

## When to Use
- Opening a new engineering role and designing the job description
- Evaluating candidates through a structured interview process
- Improving an existing hiring process that isn't attracting the right candidates

## Workflow
1. Define the role clearly: responsibilities, level expectations, must-have vs nice-to-have skills
2. Write a job description focused on impact and growth, not just requirements
3. Design interview panels with balanced assessments: technical, systems, behavioral
4. Use rubrics for consistent evaluation — score before discussing as a group
5. Collect structured debrief notes immediately after each interview

## Quality Bar
- Job descriptions attract diverse candidates (avoid gendered or exclusionary language)
- Hiring process is predictable: candidates know what to expect at each stage
- Evaluation rubrics are calibrated across interviewers before hiring begins
- Hire decisions are based on documented evidence, not gut feel
