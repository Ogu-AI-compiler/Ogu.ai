# Technical Leadership

## When to Use
- Making or reviewing significant architectural decisions
- Resolving technical disagreements within or across teams
- Setting quality standards and engineering practices for a team

## Workflow
1. Define the technical vision: where should we be in 12-18 months and why
2. Use RFCs for significant decisions: document problem, options, trade-offs, recommendation
3. Build consensus: hold architecture reviews with relevant stakeholders before deciding
4. Communicate decisions with context: why this option, what trade-offs were accepted
5. Measure outcomes: track whether architectural decisions achieve their stated goals

## Quality Bar
- Decisions are documented with reasoning, not just the outcome
- Trade-offs acknowledged explicitly — no decision is free of trade-offs
- Junior engineers are included and mentored through the process
- Decisions are revisited when the context changes
